THE DOMINO-EFFECT

How even your last act can change a part of the world

Do•mi•no-effect

Noun

1. the situation in which one event causes a series of related events, one following another

One day in the late 1800s...

... a body was pulled from the River Seine at the Quai du Louvre
The body belonged to a young girl, no older than 16
Her skin was so flawless and without damage that her death was decleared a suicide
She was put on display at ‘Unknown Paris’
This was a chilled room with a glass window where unidentified bodies were put, in the hope that someone walking by would recognise them
This was a popular attraction in Paris

Even though no one could identify ‘The unknown women of the Seine’ her appearance turned heads

Her face had something very special, she seemed to almost have a smiling, satisfied look on her face and people thought it was beautiful

One of those people was a pathologist at the Paris Morgue, who was so compelled by her beauty that he decided to immortalize it

With wax he made a mask of her face and from that point on her face became art

Thousands of copies where made

People made paintings, drawings, literature and more about this beautiful face

Years later, a Norwegian toymaker Asmund Laerdal started creating a new kind of doll

A type of doll where people could practise life-saving techniques on, now known as a CPR doll
